I am a roboticist specializing in motion planning and control for legged locomotion. I earned my Ph.D. in the Robotics and Mechanisms Laboratory (RoMeLa) at UCLA, where I worked with Prof. Dennis Hong. I am currently completing a postdoctoral position at the Georgia Institute of Technology, where I've had the opportunity to collaborate with Prof. Ye Zhao and Prof. Sam Coogan.

During my Ph.D., my research focused primarily on the hardware aspects of robotics, including the development of wall-climbing robots, grippers, and humanoids. In my postdoc, I have shifted toward more theoretical and general aspects. My current research includes (1) using Benders Decomposition—an approach well-established in the optimization and operations research community—to accelerate robotic hybrid motion planning and model predictive control (MPC), and (2) developing Logic Network Flow to accelerate task and motion planning (TAMP) under temporal logic constraints.
